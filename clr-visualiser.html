Step 2 Pupil Centre Localisation
2.1
Raw Eye Crop
Full-colour eye region extracted by Step 1
Raw Input
The eye crop arrives in RGB colour from Step 1 — already tightly bounded around the iris with 20% horizontal and 40% vertical padding. The pupil appears as the dark central circle inside the coloured iris.
RGB colour Full resolution Iris landmarks already known
2.2
Grayscale Conversion
Colour stripped — only luminance matters for circle detection
cv2.cvtColor(crop, COLOR_RGB2GRAY)
Colour information is discarded. The pupil becomes the darkest region; the sclera the brightest. This is the input to all further processing steps.
2.3
CLAHE Enhancement
Contrast boosted — dark irises become detectable
CLAHE — Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalisation
Applied with clipLimit=2.0, tileGridSize=(4,4). Brightens the iris texture while keeping the pupil dark — critical for brown or dark irises where the pupil/iris boundary would otherwise be invisible to Hough.
Fixes dark irises Applied BEFORE Hough only
2.4
Gaussian Blur
Eyelash noise smoothed before circle detection
GaussianBlur — kernel (7,7)
Eyelashes and fine iris texture create false edges that confuse Hough. A 7×7 Gaussian kernel blurs these away, leaving only the large circular boundary of the pupil/iris.
2.5
MediaPipe Iris Landmark
Mean of 5 iris points → first pupil estimate (blue)
Primary Estimate — Free from Step 1
Landmarks 468–472 (left eye) give five points around the iris boundary plus the centre. Their mean position gives the iris centre for free — no extra computation needed. Plotted here as the blue dot.
Sub-pixel precision Always available
2.6
Hough Circle Transform
Edge-based circle fit → second pupil estimate (green)
Validation Method
HoughCircles(dp=1, minDist=50, param1=50, param2=30) with radius constrained to 20–55% of crop width. Finds the best circular edge in the blurred grayscale image. Plotted as the green circle.
Independent from landmarks Can fail on very dark irises
2.7
Agreement Check → Final Centre
If estimates within 5px → HIGH confidence. Output: white cross
Confidence Decision
If Euclidean distance between blue dot and green circle centre is < 5px → HIGH confidence, use averaged position.

If distance 5–15px → MEDIUM, average both.

If Hough failed entirely → LOW, landmark only, flag pupil_disagreement.
Step 3 CLR Bright-Spot Detection
3.1
Eye Crop with CLR
Raw image — bright corneal reflex spot visible on iris
What You're Looking For
The phone torch (held co-axially with the camera) creates a tiny specular reflection on the cornea surface. It appears as the single brightest spot in the entire eye crop — distinctly brighter than any other feature.
Always the brightest region Requires flash/torch enabled
3.2
97th Percentile Threshold
Adaptive cutoff — top 3% brightest pixels selected
np.percentile(gray, 97)
Rather than a fixed value like 240/255, the threshold adapts to the actual image brightness. In dim environments the threshold might be 180; in bright ones 240. This is what makes the detection robust across different lighting conditions.
Self-adapting Fails if no flash: max pixel < 240
3.3
Binary Threshold Mask
Everything below threshold → black. CLR → white blob
cv2.threshold — Binary Mask
All pixels at or above the 97th percentile become pure white (255); everything else becomes pure black (0). The result is a binary mask where white blobs represent candidate CLR regions and any other specular highlights.
3.4
Connected Component Analysis
White blobs labelled — each gets area, centroid, bounding box
cv2.connectedComponentsWithStats
Each contiguous group of white pixels becomes a labelled blob with its own area, centroid (x,y), and bounding box stats. Multiple blobs can exist — eyelid edges, glasses reflections, and the true CLR all produce candidates here.
Multiple blobs expected Centroid per blob
3.5
3-Way Filter
Location ✕ Area ✕ Circularity — rejects eyelash/glasses noise
Three Simultaneous Constraints
① Location: Blob centroid must be within central 80% of crop width and height — rules out eyelid-edge highlights.

② Area: Must be 0.5%–15% of iris area — too small = noise, too large = flash artifact.

③ Circularity: 4π·area / perimeter² > 0.5 — the corneal reflex is nearly circular; eyelash reflections are not.
Location check Area check Circularity check
3.6
CLR Centroid Located
Largest valid blob → sub-pixel centroid marked in amber
Output: (clr_x, clr_y)
The largest blob that passes all three filters is selected. Its centroid becomes the CLR position — plotted here as the amber dot. This coordinate feeds directly into Step 4 where its displacement from the pupil centre is measured.

If no blob passes: flag no_reflex_left/right → INCONCLUSIVE.
Sub-pixel centroid No valid blob → INCONCLUSIVE
